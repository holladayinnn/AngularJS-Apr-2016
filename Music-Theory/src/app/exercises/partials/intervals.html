Relative Pitch Interval Ear Training Game

Instructions on how to play:

Click the "Play Interval" button to hear two notes that will be played either ascending, descending, or simultaneously.

Your task is to identify what the interval is between the two notes.

Each piano key represents a different interval, i.e. the low C would represent a unison interval and the adjacent D would represent a major 2nd interval. If you select the right piano key, your score will increase. If you select the wrong piano key, you will lose a life. Either way you will progress to the next question after answering.

The piano keys in this game do not represent the actual notes being played. The idea isto identify the pitches relative to each other, not the actual notes.

You have lives. If you lose all of your lives you will have to start over.

After questions, if you do not lose your lives you will go the next level!

As the level changes, different intervals will be played (look on the left of the piano to see which ones). The higher level, the higher the score you get per question.

You are also welcome to select any level you want, but your score will reset to 0, and you will not be able to get as high of a score as if you started the game from the beginning.

If you get one of the top ten scores and save a username on the top of the page you will be added to the leaderboard page.

Have fun!
