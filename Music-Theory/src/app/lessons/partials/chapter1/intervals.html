Introduction to Intervals

An interval is the difference between two notes or pitches. Intervals, along with steps, are the building blocks of melodies, scales and chords.

You can measure intervals using whole steps and half steps. Interval names are usually associated with numbers such as seconds, thirds, fourths, fifths, sixths, and so on depending on how big the difference is between the two notes.

Examples: The interval name of two notes a half step apart, such as E and F, is a minor second. The interval name of two notes a whole step apart, such as C and D, is a major second. The interval name of two notes a whole and a half step apart, such as E and G, is a minor third. The interval name of two notes that are two whole steps apart, such as G and B, is a major third.

Do not worry about understanding exactly how intervals are named yet. I will explain in a future lesson.

You can describe intervals as being consonant or dissonant. These are subjective terms to describe whether you think the two notes in the interval sound harmonious/agreeable when played together (consonant) or conflicting/tense when played together (dissonant).
