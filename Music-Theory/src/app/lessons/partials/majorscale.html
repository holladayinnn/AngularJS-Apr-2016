The Major Scale

The major scale, known modally as the ionian scale, is one of the most important scales in western music. The major scale is a seven note scale and therefore referred to as a heptatonic scale.

The C major scale is the only major scale with no sharps or flats, and it consists of the notes ascending and descending: [C, D, E, F, G, A, B]. If you play all of the white notes on the piano from C to C an octave higher, you will be playing the C major scale.

The formula for a major scale is whole step, whole step, half step, whole step, whole step, whole step, half step. For example, in the C major scale, C to D is a whole step, D to E is a whole step, E to F is a half step, F to G is a whole step, G to A is a whole step, A to B is a whole step, and B to C is a half step.

You can use this major scale formula to put the scale in other keys. If you wanted to get the major scale in the key D for example, you would start on D and use the same formula: [WS, WS, HS, WS, WS, WS, HS]. The D major scale consists of the notes: [D, E, F#, G, A, B, C#]. The note of the key you are in (in this case D) is called the tonic, the root, or the tonal center.

You might also recognize this scale as Do, Re, Mi, Fa, So, La, Ti, Do.
