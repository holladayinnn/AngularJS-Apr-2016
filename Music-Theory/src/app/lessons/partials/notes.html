The Notes

A note represents a pitch, or a certain frequency.

In Western Music, we base the names of the notes on the first 7 letters of the alphabet: A, B, C, D, E, F, G.

In addition to these 7 notes (which are called the natural notes), we have 5 notes in between. These notes in between are called the sharps and flats. Each of these notes has two names. For example, the note in between C and D can be called either C-sharp (notated C#) or D-flat (notated Db).

Therefore, all together we have a total of 12 notes. If we look at the piano below, we can see all 12 notes: 7 white notes (the natural notes) and 5 black notes (the sharps and flats).

Note: On the piano, it is common to use C as the reference point instead of A, so the first note on this piano is C. After G, the next natural note is A. Also notice that as you click the piano keys from left to right, the pitch gets higher.
